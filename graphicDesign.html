Graphic Design Projects

Wings

For this project, I used processing and data to create a wearable object that signifies my dream of seeing my parents and friends abroad at will. The idea is that each feather symbolizes my relationship with a friend or family member. The width of each diagonal is determined by how far the person lives away from me and the time difference between our cities.

Home Sweet Home

A public intervention on Penn's campus, this lenticular poster explores the theme of academic pressure. On their way in, students are put in the perspective of "Home Sweet Home," which could be interpreted with a sarcastic or playful tone. On their way in, the perspective changes to underscore the efforts students put into their work and time spent in the building.

The Last Selfie

The objective of this student project was to design a unique street sign. Noticing how often people take selfies, I decided to exaggerate the phenomenon by making a warning sign of falling off the cliff accidentally for his selfie and ironically still laughing in the photo.

The Abyss

This design was loosely based on the idea of despair. The image could be interpreted as the wing flying out of the abyss or as it failing to escape the abyss and shattering into little pieces.

About The Artist

Born in China in 1996 and moved to Los Angeles at the age of eleven, Claire aspires to explore her multicultural background by means of digital illustration and design.
