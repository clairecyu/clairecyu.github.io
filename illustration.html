Illustrations

Three Animals

This is a pair of illustrations inspired by the three types of animals: doves, wolves, and red pandas. While the first depicts a mythical creature formed by parts of each of the chosen animals, the second is a study of them placed in an imaginary landscape.

The Girl Underwater

A study from photograph, this illustration is my first attempt at drawing with a more realistic style. I focused on adjusting my brushstroke to distinguish between the character's body above and under water. It was a valuable experience for me to stay away from overrelying on contour.

The Forbidden Palace

This illustration depicts the Forbidden Palace of China with an imaginary background. It was inspired by my trip to the Forbidden Palace last year, during which I was amazed by the color scheme of the architecture. The overwhelming presence of bright red paint was surprisingly harmonious with other vibrant colors. Therefore, I decided to challenge myself and try out a more vibrant palatte in This illustration.

Album Cover Design

These are the my mock album cover designs for the Neighbourhood's first album, I Love You. Given the irony between its angsty content and its cliche title, my design uses the play-on words- eye, heart, you- to emphasize the name and also the nature of the songs.

The Hateful Eight

This is a series of illustrations for the Tarantino movie, The Hateful Eight. The background colors and arrangement are chosen based on the order of the characters' demise in the movie.

The Modern Ten Commandments

This is a mock editorial illustration for the article "Thou Shalt Update These Super-Old Commandments." To best suit the content of the article, I illustrated a modern version of Jesus to highlight the satirical nature of the article.

Penn Appetite Fish Chart

Another editorial piece, this is a illustration of a fish chart for the most common commercial fish. It was published on Penn Appetite to accompany an article on how to distinguish between different tyepes of salmons and trouts.

The Peanut Butter Fanatic

This is an earlier work of mine on character design, which depicts a little boy bathing in peanut butter. It was inspired by my friend's crazy addiction to peanut butter. As one of my earlier works, this illustration is of the Japanese manga style.

The Cherry Blossom Cake

Another experiment on a more painterly style, this illustration depicts a wedding cake. It was my first time illustrating food and I must say, trying out various methods to depict the change in texture was certainly a challenge.

The Stranger

This is a triptych depicting three scenes in Camus's book, the Stranger. The use of black and white linework emphasizes the sinister nature of the story. The filters were chosen based on the mood of the protagonist at each specific point of his life.

About The Artist

Born in China in 1996 and moved to Los Angeles at the age of eleven, Claire aspires to explore her multicultural background by means of digital illustration and design.
